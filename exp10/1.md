## 2. Methodology

### 2.1 Self2Self (Bernoulli Sampling + Dropout)
Self2Self builds on the idea that an image can be reconstructed from its sub-sampled versions. 
- **Bernoulli Sampling**: The input noisy image is randomly sampled using a Bernoulli mask to create different instances.
- **Dropout**: The network uses dropout during training to prevent overfitting to the noise.
- **Ensemble Inference**: At test time, multiple predictions are made with different dropout masks and averaged to obtain the final denoised result.

### 2.2 Deep Image Prior (DIP)
DIP demonstrates that the architecture of a convolutional neural network (CNN) itself acts as a powerful image prior.
- **Fixed Random Input**: The network takes a fixed random noise vector as input.
- **Optimization**: The network is optimized to reconstruct the single noisy target image.
- **Early Stopping**: Because CNNs tend to learn low-frequency structural information before high-frequency noise, stopping the optimization early results in a denoised version of the target.

## 5. Analysis and Discussion

### 5.1 Comparison of Mechanisms
- **Self2Self** relies on the statistical redundancy of the image through Bernoulli sampling and ensemble averaging. By training on masked instances and using Dropout at inference time, it effectively approximates the clean signal. In our tests, it achieved results very close to Neighbor2Neighbor, demonstrating high robustness for single-image restoration.
- **DIP** relies on the "inductive bias" of the network structure itself. It requires no training data other than the noisy target. While highly flexible, it is sensitive to the optimization length; stopping too late causes it to fit the noise (overfitting). Its PSNR is slightly lower than Self2Self but remarkably high considering it uses no external data.

### 5.2 Performance vs. Data Requirements
The progression of experiments (exp7 $\to$ exp10) shows that while supervised learning (exp7) provides the upper bound on performance, self-supervised methods like **Self2Self** and **DIP** are now capable of achieving practical denoising quality (within 1-2 dB of the supervised baseline) using only the single noisy image. This makes them ideal for specialized imaging fields where clean ground truth is impossible to collect.
